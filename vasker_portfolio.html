Curriculum Reform and Teacher Motivation: The Case of Bangladesh

Vasker Dey & Dr. Mohammad Mahboob Morshed
📖 Peer-Reviewed 🌏 International Journal 🏛 University of Dhaka
Abstract

This study investigates the relationship between ongoing curriculum reform initiatives in Bangladesh and teacher motivation at the secondary level. Drawing on qualitative data from in-depth interviews and classroom observations, the study reveals that while many teachers acknowledge the pedagogical rationale behind the reforms, implementation is frequently undermined by inadequate professional development, unclear communication of reform objectives, and perceived disconnect between policy design and classroom realities. The paper argues for reform models that centre teacher agency and invest in sustained capacity-building to drive genuine motivational alignment.
